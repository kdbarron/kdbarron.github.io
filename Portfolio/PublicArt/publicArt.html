Engagement Eggs

A product to get residents more involved with public art in their community, as well as learn more about the history of the art.

My Role

I was the lead researcher for this project. I was in charge of reaching out to local artists in pittsburgh who created public art, as well as ensure the quality of our research in the field was above and beyond expectations

Project Overview

Our project looked at how we could create an engaging way to attract people to public art in the Pittsburgh area. We conducted several research sessions and from our synthesis came up with the idea of Engagement Eggs. These eggs would be placed outside of public art displays and would contain information about the art piece.

Research Methods

Our team used three different generative research methods, including Observe & Intercept, Think-Aloud Protocol, and Semi-Structured Interviews. We also used evaluative methods, including Speeddating, and experience prototyping.

Going Forward

We would like to continue measuring engagement with public art by creating a social media account which people could post their collection of eggs to. Ideally we would have multiple egg locations, so that people would have to engage in a scavenger hunt for all different kinds of eggs.
